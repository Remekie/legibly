FAQ

Common questions.

Why doesn't my site show up in ChatGPT or Perplexity?

Usually one of three reasons: (1) Your site is built with a JavaScript framework and AI crawlers can't read it without a browser, (2) you're blocking AI crawlers in your site's settings, or (3) your site has no structured information that tells AI what your business does. Legibly checks all three in 30 seconds.

What's the difference between AI search and regular Google search?

Google runs JavaScript and indexes your site like a browser would. AI crawlers — GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot — do not. They read raw HTML only. A site that ranks well on Google can be completely invisible to AI if it relies on JavaScript to display its content.

I built my site with Lovable / Bolt / v0. Is that a problem?

Yes — by default. These tools build React apps that require JavaScript to display content. When an AI crawler visits, it sees an empty page. Legibly's $149 fix automatically resolves this by adding a pre-rendering step that serves real HTML to AI crawlers, without changing how your site looks to human visitors.

What does the $79 report include?

A full diagnostic: the prompts your customers are typing where competitors appear instead of you, copy-paste structured data code for your site, a generated AI guide file (llms.txt), a check of whether your business appears in AI answers, and a downloadable PDF of everything.

How does the $149 fix work?

You connect your GitHub repository. We detect what your site is built with, generate all the fixes, and open a pull request — a ready-to-approve code change. Your developer (or you, if you built with Lovable) reviews and approves it. Your site rebuilds with everything fixed. We then re-scan your site 30 days later to confirm your grade improved.

Do I need to be technical to use Legibly?

No. The free scan requires only your URL. The $79 report requires only your email and a credit card. The $149 fix requires access to your site's code repository — if you don't have that, you can forward the report to your developer and they can implement everything from the instructions.
